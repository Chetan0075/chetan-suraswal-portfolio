The Problem

Revenue Was Healthy.
Profit Wasn't the Whole Story.

Revenue trends alone hide where margin is actually being made or lost. A category can grow in volume while bleeding profit through discounting, and a region can look strong on the top line while specific product lines quietly drag it down. The business needed visibility into profit, not just sales — broken down by category, region, and time period, not just totals.

The Dataset

Retail Sales Data,
Order-Level Detail.

Sourced from a Kaggle retail sales dataset — order-level transactions spanning multiple product categories, regions, and time periods, including discount and shipping cost fields needed to calculate true profit rather than gross revenue.

Data Quality & Assumptions

  • Profit calculated post-discount and shipping, not gross revenue
  • Returned orders excluded from profit metrics to avoid double-counting losses
  • Regional demand seasonality not fully captured in the current model

The Approach

Five Stages,
Raw Data to Revenue Insight.

01

Raw Sales Data

Started with order-level retail transactions across regions, categories, and time periods — the full, unfiltered transaction history.

02

Data Cleaning

Removed duplicate orders, standardized date and currency fields, and excluded returned orders from profit calculations so reversed sales couldn't distort the margin picture.

03

Profit Modeling

Calculated true profit per order after discount and shipping cost — instead of relying on gross revenue, which hides exactly where margin is leaking.

04

Category Analysis

Broke profit down by category and region to find where the small set of high-performing SKUs — and the loss-making ones — were actually concentrated.

05

Revenue Insights

Packaged the findings into a live Power BI dashboard, so category and regional teams can check margin health without waiting on a quarterly report.

What It Found

Insights & Decisions.

Key Business Insights

  • High-discount orders erode margins without improving volume
  • 18% of SKUs generate 60%+ of total profit
  • Category-level loss concentrated in specific regions

Decisions Enabled

  • Flagged high-discount, low-margin order patterns for review
  • Reallocated marketing spend toward high-profit SKUs
  • Surfaced underperforming category-region pairs for pricing review
